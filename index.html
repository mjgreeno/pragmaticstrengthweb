pragmatic
/ prag · ˈma · tik /
adjective
1. Dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a way that is based on practical rather than theoretical considerations.
a pragmatic approach to strength — built on what works, not what's popular.
2. Focused on results; guided by experience and evidence over ideology.
pragmatic athletes train with intention, compete with confidence, and improve relentlessly.
synonyms: practical  ·  disciplined  ·  results-driven  ·  sensible  ·  grounded  ·  purposeful

Our Ethos

The Pragmatic Philosophy

Silver bullets are for warewolves not strength athletes. We will fail and we will succeed, but we will always stay rooted in the belief so we may achieve our goals.

"

Strength is derived from belief. It is a lesson — that is built rep by rep, meet by meet, year by year. At Pragmatic Strength, we believe the most powerful thing a coach can do is help an athlete understand why they train the way they train. So they may may lead with conviction.

— The Pragmatic Strength Coaching Standard
01

You Are Number 1

Every relationship, program and decision is grounded in Honesty, be true to yourself and your own journey. be true to your coach and your fellow athletes. Be true to the process and the journey.

02

Be True to the people you are with

No journey is best done alone. We are all in this together, and we all have something to learn from each other. Be true to the people you are with, and the people you are with you. they will carry you when it counts.

03

Reality First

Strength is a an escape its not our profession its our passion. Use the same passion for strength in everything you do not just in the gym. Goals are all around us achieve them all on all plains of existence.

04

Lead with Conviction

You are an elaborate tapestry of experiences, emotions, and thoughts. Lead with conviction, not with ego. strength is but one small pattern in the fabric of your life. there is so much more to you never forget that.
